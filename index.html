What is a Genetic Disease?

Genetic diseases occur due to mutations in DNA sequences, which may lead to conditions such as sickle cell anemia, cystic fibrosis, and Huntington's disease. Identifying these mutations early can help with diagnosis and treatment.

How Proteins Play a Role

Proteins, synthesized from DNA instructions, are crucial for cellular functions. Mutations can affect protein structure, leading to diseases. Understanding protein interactions helps in disease research.
